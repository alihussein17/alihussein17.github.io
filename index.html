Boeing 777: The Iconic Airplane

The Boeing 777 is a long-range wide-body twin-engine jet airliner developed by Boeing Commercial Airplanes. It is the world's largest twinjet and has a typical seating capacity of 314 to 396 passengers. The Boeing 777 was first introduced in 1995 and has been one of the most successful planes in the aviation industry.

The aircraft is renowned for its impressive range, fuel efficiency, and safety record, making it a favorite among many airlines. From passenger travel to cargo, the Boeing 777 has revolutionized air travel.
